A Continent Getting Hotter

Australia has warmed by approximately 1.4°C since national records began in 1910. Most of this warming has occurred after 1950, driven by rising greenhouse gas emissions. Cool years are becoming rarer — and record-breaking heat is becoming the norm.

Disasters Are Increasing

The number of recorded natural disasters has grown significantly since the 1990s. While improved record-keeping plays a role, the data reflects a real and alarming increase in the frequency of extreme weather events across Australia.

Disasters Across the Nation

No state is immune. New South Wales and Victoria bear the heaviest disaster burden, while Queensland faces frequent cyclones and floods. The southeast corner of Australia — home to most of the population — is also the most disaster-prone region.

Breaking down disasters by type, floods and bushfires dominate — and both have surged dramatically since the 1990s as temperatures have climbed.

The Human Cost

Some disasters have left permanent scars on Australia's history. Cyclone Mahina remains the deadliest on record, while the 2009 Black Saturday bushfires are the deadliest in modern times. These events remind us that natural disasters are not just statistics — they are human tragedies.

Which Disasters Are Getting Worse?

This heatmap reveals how each disaster type has intensified over the decades. Storms and bushfires have seen the sharpest increases since the 1990s, reflecting a continent increasingly at the mercy of extreme weather.

The Billion Dollar Toll

The financial cost of natural disasters has exploded in recent decades. The 2010s alone saw over $16 billion in insured losses — more than all previous decades combined. As disasters intensify, so does the economic burden on communities and insurers.

The Climate Connection

The link between rising temperatures and increasing disasters is clear. As Australia's temperature anomaly grows, so does the number of recorded disasters each year. The darker dots — representing more recent years — cluster in the upper right, showing the accelerating trend.

What Threatens Australia Most?

Floods are the most common natural disaster in Australia, followed closely by bushfires and cyclones. Together these three categories account for the vast majority of all recorded disaster events.
